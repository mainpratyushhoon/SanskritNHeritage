Methodology

The research and development workflow used to build the Sanskrit Knowledge Ecosystem website, including source selection, verification, and content organization.

Research Workflow

We adopted a structured, learner-centric approach to develop the Sanskrit resource platform, focusing on reducing complexity and improving accessibility. The process began with defining clear user segments beginner, intermediate, and advanced learner to ensure content relevance at each stage. Instead of creating content from scratch, we prioritized curated aggregation by identifying high-quality existing resources such as videos, texts, and tools, and systematically filtering them based on clarity, credibility, and usability. These resources were then organized into a layered learning framework, progressing from foundational concepts like the Devanagari script and basic vocabulary to advanced grammar, application, and literature. The platform was designed with an intuitive and minimal user interface, emphasizing guided learning paths, integrated practice exercises, and easy navigation. Continuous iteration was incorporated by planning feedback loops and scalability features such as progress tracking and community engagement. This methodology ensured that the platform functions not merely as a repository of resources, but as a cohesive and guided learning ecosystem for Sanskrit.
