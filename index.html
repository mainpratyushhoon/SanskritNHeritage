5,000 Years of Civilization

The heritage of Sanskrit

✦
c. 1500 BCE
The Rigveda — World's Oldest Verse
The Rigveda is composed, making it the oldest known Indo-European religious text and one of humanity's earliest literary monuments.
c. 400 BCE
Panini's Ashtadhyayi
Panini writes the world's first formal grammar, codifying Classical Sanskrit in 3,976 sutras — a feat of linguistic precision unmatched for centuries.
c. 300 BCE – 500 CE
Spread Across Asia
Sanskrit travels with Buddhist and Hindu missionaries to Southeast Asia, Central Asia, and China, becoming the lingua franca of scholarship and faith.
c. 1786 CE
Sir William Jones & Comparative Linguistics
Jones's discovery of Sanskrit's connection to Greek and Latin founds modern comparative linguistics and reveals the Indo-European language family.
